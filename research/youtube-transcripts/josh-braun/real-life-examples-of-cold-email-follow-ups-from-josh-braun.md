## Why This Source Was Collected

Josh Braun is useful for this project because he focuses on buyer psychology, resistance, and practical message framing. His content is less about automation and more about how a cold message feels to the person receiving it.

## Key Ideas

- Follow-up should create a reason to continue the conversation, not simply ask whether the prospect saw the previous email.
- Useful follow-ups can add context, share a resource, clarify a problem, or introduce a fresh angle.
- Buyers resist when the message feels like a pitch before trust or relevance has been established.
- A good follow-up can be short while still being specific.

## Useful Playbook Implications

- Write follow-ups as new value moments, not reminders.
- Avoid "just checking in" language.
- Keep the buyer's problem visible in every touch.
- Use follow-ups to make the next step easier, not to pressure the buyer.
